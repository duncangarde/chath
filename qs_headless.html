Political Political or Policy relates to leadership and establishing policies for cybersecurity strategy, planning, and risk assessment strategies in an organization. It involves implementing regulation and national guidelines for information and cyber security and setting up pro- active approaches to mitigate cyber risk.

The leadership in the organization should be able to see the bigger security environment and existing and emerging threats. The leadership’s role is to map out risks and threats and decide on possible investment on cybersecurity measures.
Political questions to ask +×
Is cybersecurity considered at the highest level possible?
Are policies and working practices updated regularly? +×
Updates should follow significant incidents or installation of new ICT architectures.
Economic It is important for the nuclear industry to realize the costs associated in under investment in cybersecurity. Economic calculations should address who is taking the risk: is it the nuclear operators or is it underwritten by a third party such as the nuclear insurers? The insurance sector could foster awareness and create financial incentives for the businesses (e.g. discounted premiums for mature cyber security culture and behaviour).

Societal The social factors relate to the issues with regards to the organizational culture, which involves establishing vetting and human resource procedures; tackling insider threat issues, setting up risk communication strategies, among others. Good social practices involve developing training (e.g. table-top exercises, simulations), education and public awareness strategies.

Technological As technology changes rapidly, industry-level cybersecurity practices will start to incorporate emerging technology solutions into their business analysis. Technology is an enabler for business operations but it may also create unintentional cybersecurity risks.

The questions below have been developed as best practice guidelines for the C-Suite (CEO/CFO) in the nuclear sector. The questions collectively aim at addressing a mature cyber security culture within the civil nuclear sector and to assist all those with responsibility for cyber security in the nuclear industry.
