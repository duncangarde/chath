Political Factors Political or Policy relates to leadership and establishing policies for cybersecurity strategy, planning, and risk assessment strategies in an organization. It involves implementing regulation and national guidelines for information and cyber security and setting up pro- active approaches to mitigate cyber risk.

The leadership in the organization should be able to see the bigger security environment and existing and emerging threats. The leadership’s role is to map out risks and threats and decide on possible investment on cybersecurity measures.
Political questions to ask +×
YNN/A Is cybersecurity considered at the highest level possible?
YNN/A Are policies and working practices updated regularly?
Updates should follow significant incidents or installation of new ICT architectures.

Economic Factors It is important for the nuclear industry to realize the costs associated in under investment in cybersecurity. Economic calculations should address who is taking the risk: is it the nuclear operators or is it underwritten by a third party such as the nuclear insurers? The insurance sector could foster awareness and create financial incentives for the businesses (e.g. discounted premiums for mature cyber security culture and behaviour).

YNN/AIs cybersecurity considered at the highest level possible?
YNN/AAre cybersecurity issues on the corporate risk register?
YNN/ADo decision makers understand where business vulnerabilities lie?
YNN/AIs the senior level aware and understands cyber dependencies?
YNN/ADoes ‘cyber’ factor into related due diligence assessments in business change such as in M&A valuations?
YNN/ADoes the organization understand how to quantify risk, particularly related to company value, share price and reputation?
YNN/AAre critical business relationships identified and graded according to the effect they would have if their provision of service was lost?
YNN/AAre appropriate corporate resources devoted to cyber security issues once vulnerabilities are recognized?
YNN/ADoes the organization actively consider terminating business relationships with suppliers due to non-compliance with IT risk management standards?
YNN/A Does the organization assess potential cyber risks in varying degrees?
Risks should be assessed at a short (six months), medium (one year) or long-term (two-five years) basis.
YNN/A Does the organization identify the corporate material and resources that must be protected at all costs?
Ensure these materials are reviewed consistently.
YNN/A Are cyber dependencies mapped, including those of corporate partners?
These dependencies should be reviewed regularly.
YNN/A Is the organization’s supply chain assessed for cyber risk, and are there audits?
Audits should be on-site for critical suppliers and non-critical suppliers can self-certify.
YNN/AAre those assessment results raised at board level?
YNN/AHas the organization dedicated vulnerability assessment and remediation staff full time?
YNN/A Do these assessments impact technology or service procurement in a fiscal manner?
This can include preferred supplier lists or discounts in fees due to un-remediated vulnerabilities in products.
YNN/ADoes the security team have a dashboard with security metrics that the board oversees?
YNN/ADoes the board review incident metrics, and conduct analysis of major security incidents?
YNN/AIs an incident response plan published internally?
YNN/AIs vulnerability assessment regular, and does it have board visibility and accountability?
YNN/A Does the organization manage incidents regularly?
It should also have a programme to reduce the cost and impact of security incidents.
YNN/AIs the culture of security assessments an iterative exercise of continuous improvement, rather than a blame culture?

Societal Factors The social factors relate to the issues with regards to the organizational culture, which involves establishing vetting and human resource procedures; tackling insider threat issues, setting up risk communication strategies, among others. Good social practices involve developing training (e.g. table-top exercises, simulations), education and public awareness strategies.
Societal questions to ask +×
YNN/AIs there a cyber security strategy within the organization?
YNN/A Do the CEO and CFO understand their cyber dependencies?
They should also know where to find appropriate guidance regarding risk mitigation.
YNN/A Is there a continuous dialogue between CISO and the board?
CISO and the board should speak the same ‘language’ in terms of risk management.
YNN/AIs there co-operation with similar organizations, in order to develop collective protection measures?
YNN/ADoes the enterprise carry out cyber security exercises from board down to the ‘shop floor’?
YNN/AAre technological trends monitored and reported to the board, encapsulating risk?
YNN/A Is the organization’s supply chain assessed for cyber risk, and are there audits?
Audits should be on-site for critical suppliers and non-critical suppliers can self-certify.
YNN/AAre lessons learned passed onto the workforce by 'information push'?
YNN/AAre revised strategies and procedures tested, via table-top, scenario exercising or virtual environments?
YNN/AAre those assessment results raised at board level?
YNN/ADoes the organization’s staff have an ongoing relationship with the vulnerability management or incident response teams of major technology suppliers?
YNN/ADo the safety and security teams meet regularly to discuss the inter-relationship between their respective work?
YNN/AIs the organization a member of threat sharing groups?
YNN/AIs an incident response plan published internally?
YNN/AAre indicators of compromise shared with other nuclear facilities and the supply chain?
YNN/AIs the security team full-time, trained to a high standard, and does it regularly collaborate with the safety team?
YNN/AIs the incident response team a member of FIRST.org or TF-CSIRT?
YNN/AAre threat intelligence and indicators of compromise shared with trusted vetted groups similar to the organization?
YNN/AIs vulnerability assessment regular, and does it have board visibility and accountability?
YNN/ADoes extensive security training of all staff occur regularly?
YNN/AIs the culture of security assessments an iterative exercise of continuous improvement, rather than a blame culture?
YNN/ACan the organization put a cost/benefit on security in its supply chain and actively manage supplier relationships?

Technological Factors As technology changes rapidly, industry-level cybersecurity practices will start to incorporate emerging technology solutions into their business analysis. Technology is an enabler for business operations but it may also create unintentional cybersecurity risks.

The questions below have been developed as best practice guidelines for the C-Suite (CEO/CFO) in the nuclear sector. The questions collectively aim at addressing a mature cyber security culture within the civil nuclear sector and to assist all those with responsibility for cyber security in the nuclear industry.
Technological questions to ask +×
YNN/A Is there a single person in the institution whose sole responsibility is to raise security awareness?
This individual should be responsible for raising security awareness for both employees and external clients.
YNN/AAre appropriate corporate resources devoted to cyber security issues once vulnerabilities are recognized?
YNN/A Does the organization assess potential cyber risks in varying degrees?
Risks should be assessed at a short (six months), medium (one year) or long-term (two-five years) basis.
YNN/AAre technological trends monitored and reported to the board, encapsulating risk?
YNN/AAre revised strategies and procedures tested, via table-top, scenario exercising or virtual environments?
YNN/AAre those tests aligned with IAEA guidance on Conducting Computer Security Assessments at Nuclear Facilities?
YNN/AAre those assessment results raised at board level?
YNN/AHas the organization dedicated vulnerability assessment and remediation staff full time?
YNN/ADoes the organization include vulnerability assessment or penetration testing in the procurement of new technologies?
YNN/A Do these assessments impact technology or service procurement in a fiscal manner?
This can include preferred supplier lists or discounts in fees due to un-remediated vulnerabilities in products.
YNN/ADo individuals in the safety team hold security certification and vice versa?
YNN/ADoes the organization use threat intelligence products?
YNN/ADoes the Incident Response capability CTI (Threat Led) use indicators of compromise to improve speed of response?
YNN/AAre corporate and industrial/safety critical networks segregated in alignment with IEC62443 zones and conduits?
YNN/AAre log monitoring systems used in OT systems?
YNN/ADoes the security team have a dashboard with security metrics that the board oversees?
YNN/A Does the organization perform Secure Development Life Cycle assurance for systems procurement?
This should be carried out for both internal and external suppliers.
YNN/ADoes the organization have a security incident response team?
YNN/AIs the team structured as per the guidance in the IAEA Incident Response Planning at Nuclear Facilities?
YNN/ADoes the board review incident metrics, and conduct analysis of major security incidents?
YNN/AIs an incident response plan published internally?
YNN/AAre indicators of compromise shared with other nuclear facilities and the supply chain?
YNN/AIs the security team full-time, trained to a high standard, and does it regularly collaborate with the safety team?
YNN/AIs the incident response team a member of FIRST.org or TF-CSIRT?
YNN/AAre threat intelligence and indicators of compromise shared with trusted vetted groups similar to the organization?
YNN/AIs vulnerability assessment regular, and does it have board visibility and accountability?
YNN/A Does the organization manage incidents regularly?
It should also have a programme to reduce the cost and impact of security incidents.
YNN/ACan the organization put a cost/benefit on security in its supply chain and actively manage supplier relationships?
